# Candidate-Free Improvement for Clean-Election Forecasts

For clean elections, replace the direct pooled win classifier with a **fundamentals-and-swing model**.  Within each election type, predict the governing party or coalition's change in vote/seat share relative to its prior comparable election, then convert that predicted swing (and its uncertainty) into an incumbent-retention probability using the country's electoral-system rules.  The model should partially pool country history with comparable democracies—grouped by presidential/parliamentary system, PR/majoritarian rules, and government/coalition status—rather than treating every country as either wholly independent or identical.  Inputs remain candidate-free and strictly pre-election: lagged incumbent share and margin, time in office, coalition fragmentation, economic change versus the country's own recent baseline, inflation/unemployment shocks, institutional constraints, and prior turnout/volatility.

This targets the main difficulty of clean elections: outcomes are usually decided by relatively small, country-specific swings, while a raw global incumbent-win label hides both the baseline advantage and the electoral threshold.  Modeling the swing makes a 3-point loss in a normally dominant party different from the same loss in a marginal government, and partial pooling supplies useful signal where a country has few observations without discarding its history.  Estimating uncertainty and calibrating probabilities separately for executive and legislative races should reduce overconfident calls; rolling, time-ordered validation must test every transformation using only information available before each election.  The approach preserves the project's structural, non-candidate scope while making its clean-election predictions better aligned with how democratic incumbency is won or lost.
